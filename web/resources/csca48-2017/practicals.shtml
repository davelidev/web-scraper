Practicals are drop-in sessions that run throughout the week. You can attend for as much or as little time as you think is beneficial to you. TAs will be present during this time to help students with the practical materials, as well as any other course related questions they may have.

During practicals, points will be awarded on the Tracademic system by the TAs for attendance (Experience points), completing challenge questions (Challenge points), and helping fellow students (Teaching points).

Anti-lectures are drop-in sessions that are designed to help students who were unable to attend a lecture or tutorial, or who did attend the lecture/tutorial, but felt that they weren't able to fully grasp the material (we all have off days, some times nothing sinks in). The anti-lectures assume that you have read through the lecture/tutorial notes and have an idea of what was covered. The instructor running the session will not be re-teaching the material from scratch, but rather helping students to fill in any gaps they may have.

Practical Questions

Practical questions are not mandatory, and will not be marked. Challenge questions (marked in the files with a bold (Challenge) can be shown to any TA during a practical session in order to obtain challenge points.
